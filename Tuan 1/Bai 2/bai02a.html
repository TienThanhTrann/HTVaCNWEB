Leonhard Euler (1707- 1783)

Considered the most prolific mathematician of all time, Euler published nearly 900 books. Despite losing his sight in his late 50s, his capabilities across various domains only increased. Euler introduced a famous mathematical expression, a profound connection between complex exponential functions and trigonometric functions, known as Euler's identity:
